Top Stories
Strait of Hormuz Near Total Halt as Iran War Escalates
Rigzone / CNBC / Upstream Online
Maritime traffic through the Strait of Hormuz has ground to a near-complete standstill, with no oil shipments recorded in the past 24 hours as the U.S.-Israeli Operation Epic Fury against Iran enters its seventh day. Energy exporters in the Gulf are scrambling to secure alternative routing options amid fears of a prolonged global supply crunch. The Trump administration announced a $20 billion reinsurance program for oil tankers in response to the crisis, signaling recognition that commercial insurers have effectively withdrawn coverage for Hormuz transits.
Brent Tops $90, WTI Posts Record Weekly Gain on Iran War
Financial Times / CNBC / World Oil / OilPrice
Brent crude broke above $90 per barrel Friday after President Trump declared he would accept nothing short of unconditional surrender from Iran, while WTI posted its largest weekly percentage gain on record — up roughly 14% — according to market data. Oil prices are now at their highest levels since 2023, with analysts warning that the supply shock from Hormuz disruptions is outpacing any demand-side headwinds from a softening U.S. economy. The IEA said Friday it sees no immediate need to release emergency strategic reserves, a stance traders are watching closely for any shift.
Iran Drones Strike Azerbaijan; Nakhchivan Airport Terminal Hit
OilPrice
Iran launched a drone attack on Azerbaijan's Nakhchivan exclave on March 5, wounding four civilians and striking a terminal at the region's airport, with a second drone narrowly missing a school building. Azerbaijan's Defense Ministry issued a warning of an "iron fist" response, raising the risk that the Iran conflict could spread northward and draw additional regional actors into the war. The attack introduces new uncertainty over energy infrastructure and transit corridors in the South Caucasus, a corridor relevant to Caspian oil flows.
Natural Gas Futures Rise as War Risk Offsets Weak Spring Demand
NGI
Natural gas futures settled higher Friday as escalating Middle East tensions and the broader global energy price surge countered seasonally weak U.S. spring demand fundamentals. Henry Hub cash prices climbed while West Texas Waha prices sank, reflecting mixed regional dynamics including mild weather outlooks and pipeline constraints in the Permian Basin. Traders are closely monitoring whether LNG export disruption fears tied to the Iran conflict will provide a more sustained floor under prices heading into the shoulder season.
Iraqi Kurdistan Oilfield Attacked Days After Operations Suspended
Upstream Online
An oilfield in Iraqi Kurdistan came under attack just three days after operations at the site had already been suspended, compounding production losses in a region already stressed by the widening Iran conflict. The incident adds to mounting evidence that energy infrastructure across the broader Middle East is increasingly at risk as hostilities expand geographically. For crude traders, any further degradation of Kurdish export volumes — historically routed through the Iraq-Turkey pipeline — represents an additional tightening factor in an already supply-shocked market.
Market Outlook
The market is now fully in supply-shock mode, with WTI's record weekly gain of ~14% and Brent's breach of $90 reflecting a structural repricing rather than a transient geopolitical spike — the Hormuz near-shutdown has removed the world's single most critical oil chokepoint from active use, accounting for roughly 20% of global seaborne crude flows. Trump's declared stance of accepting nothing short of unconditional Iranian surrender eliminates any near-term diplomatic resolution scenario, meaning the supply disruption risk is open-ended; the $20B tanker reinsurance program is a de facto admission that commercial markets have written off Hormuz transits indefinitely. The WTI futures curve's steep backwardation — spot at $90.98 collapsing to $75.12 by August 2026 — tells traders the market sees the crunch as front-loaded, but that pricing window could extend violently if Iraqi Kurdistan export losses via the Iraq-Turkey pipeline compound Hormuz disruptions, removing a second major supply corridor simultaneously. Iran's drone strike on Azerbaijan's Nakhchivan introduces a third vector of risk: Caspian corridor disruption that could threaten BTC pipeline flows, adding tail-risk to an already fragile supply picture that markets have not yet fully priced. The IEA's Friday decision to hold strategic reserves signals a policy buffer remains in play — any reversal of that stance or coordinated SPR release would be the single most powerful downside catalyst and should be monitored as the primary near-term shock absorber. For the week ahead, watch Hormuz transit data, any Azerbaijani military response to the drone strike, and EIA inventory figures closely; a print showing accelerating draw-downs would validate $95+ Brent as the next target, while any credible de-escalation or SPR coordination could flush speculative length rapidly given the historic size of this week's move.
